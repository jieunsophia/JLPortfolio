Specification and Estimation of Spatial Interaction Models for Nonnegative Origin-destination Flows Manuscript
The gravity equation has a long-standing empirical tradition in estimating international trade flows. Despite its widespread use, however, the traditional gravity equation for Origin-Destination (OD) flows has significant limitations. First, it only captures bilateral interactions, lacking a broader multilateral context to account for third-party effects and multilateral resistance. Second, the common practice of log-transformed outcomes can lead to invalid inferences. Third, the equation is not robust to heteroscedasticity or spatially correlated errors. Addressing these issues, we propose specification and estimation of a novel spatial interaction model for nonnegative OD flows. Specifically, we integrate spatial weighting matrices and individual fixed effects within the Poisson maximum likelihood framework, and further adjust standard errors to ensure robustness against heteroscedasticity and spatial correlation in errors. Monte Carlo simulations show nice finite sample properties of our estimator, including consistency and fair nominal coverage. Finally, an empirical application using trade data finds strong evidence of spillover effects driven by geographic, economic, and political proximities. Interestingly, bilateral geographic distance was no longer a significant factor, which is a big shift from the usual findings with the traditional gravity equation.

Keywords: Origin-destination (OD) flows, gravity equation, third-party effects, multilateral resistance, log-transformation, heteroscedasticity-autocorrelation-consistent standard errors, Poisson pseudo maximum likelihood estimation.
