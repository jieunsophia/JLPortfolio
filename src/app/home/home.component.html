I am a PhD candidate in economics at University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, USA. My research fields are spatial econometrics and socioeconomics. Theoretically, I develop computationally efficient tests for spatial models. Notably, I model & test dependence structures that explain the endogeneity of spatial weights matrices. Empirically, I aim for interdisciplinary studies in the social sciences to find evidence for endogenous interactions constructed by non-predetermined factors, such as "economic" or "social" distances, to explain the spatial- & human interactions effects in a more valid and comprehensive manner.
